WATER: IMPACT-1

When we started this journey, we knew it would test our staying power, commitment and ingenuity. We had no strategic plan but grew organically, always responsive to the ideas and strengths of a people who depended on the effectiveness of their ideas if they were to survive and meet their own basic human needs.

At first water, food and shelter were the most pressing of these. But slowly, as people made the connection between illness and contaminated water, their needs shifted from ‘water’ to ‘safe drinking water’.

When you work by solving problems as they arise, a picture evolves, and you begin to see how tightly survival, technology and development are linked and interconnected with many aspects of people’s lives. This understanding is a great part of the Barefoot College legacy.

This ebook has traced the making of that legacy, which has taken us through a broad spectrum of activities. They have included drilling for groundwater, installing and maintaining handpumps, constructing and deepening village ponds and dams, exploring ways to rejuvenate existing wells and open water sources, making the most of rainwater by harvesting it, and constructing reverse osmosis plants in fluoride and arsenic-affected areas.

It’s been a huge criss-cross of ideas and work with communities. What began with one set of villages surrounding the Tilonia campus spread beyond Rajasthan into other Indian states, and later moved beyond India into many countries in the Global South, especially in Africa, Latin America, and Southeast Asia.

Thanks to our diligent barefoot record-keepers, counting the numbers has been easy and exciting. Watch Jokhim Chacha, the Barefoot College mascot, lead you through its narration. It may sound impressive to say that we have installed 1735 handpumps in Indian villages, or 27 community rainwater harvesting tanks in seven other countries.

But figures are only part of the story. The rest is told in the lives of the people we set out to help — the ones who live and struggle for survival every day.

Barefoot College assesses change by the improvement in people’s lives — by seeing people’s self-confidence rise, by empowerment being enabled and expectations being changed. In practice, all markers of improvement matter.
